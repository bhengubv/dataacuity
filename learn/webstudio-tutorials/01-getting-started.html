What is Web Studio?

Web Studio is like a magic art program for making websites! Instead of painting pictures, you "paint" web pages by dragging and dropping things onto your canvas.

Think of it like building with LEGO blocks - you pick the pieces you want and put them where you want them!

Fun Fact: Websites are made of special building blocks called "elements" - things like boxes, text, pictures, and buttons!
2

Opening Web Studio

Let's open Web Studio and start exploring!

Open Web Studio

When you first open it, you'll see:

  • A big blank area (your "canvas" - where you'll build your page)
  • A panel on the left with components you can add
  • A panel on the right to change how things look
  • Buttons at the top to save, preview, and publish
3

The Parts of Web Studio

The Canvas (Middle)

This is where you build your website! It starts empty, like a blank piece of paper waiting for your creativity.

Components Panel (Left Side)

This is your toolbox! It has all the building blocks you can add to your page:

  • Box - A container to group things together
  • Text - Words and paragraphs
  • Heading - Big titles for sections
  • Image - Pictures and photos
  • Button - Clickable buttons
  • Link - Text that goes somewhere when clicked

Style Panel (Right Side)

This is where you make things pretty! Change colors, sizes, and more.

4

Your First Project

Let's create your very first project!

  1. When Web Studio opens, you'll see the dashboard
  2. Click "New Project" or the + button
  3. Give your project a fun name (like "My First Website")
  4. Click "Create" and you're ready to build!
Tip: Your projects are saved automatically, so you won't lose your work!
5

Try It: Add Your First Element!

Let's add something to your blank canvas:

  1. Find "Heading" in the Components panel on the left
  2. Click and drag it onto your canvas
  3. You'll see text appear that says "Heading"
  4. Double-click on it to change the text
  5. Type something fun, like "Welcome to My Website!"
Having Trouble? Make sure you're dragging the element all the way onto the white canvas area. It should "snap" into place!

Congratulations! You just added your first element to a website! In the next lesson, we'll learn how to add more content and make it look amazing.
